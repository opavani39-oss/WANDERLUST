Country:switzerland

Switzerland is a landlocked, prosperous country in Western Europe known for its neutrality, the Alps mountains (covering 60% of the land), and high quality of life. Founded in 1291, it is a federal republic consisting of 26 cantons, with Bern as its de facto capital. It has four official languages—German, French, Italian, and Romansh—and is famous for banking, tourism, watches, and chocolate

Average Cost: $1500 - $2500

Key Cost Factors & Tips (Approx. Estimates) Flights: Roundtrip tickets range from ₹40,000 to over ₹1 lakh depending on the season, with January being cheapest and December/June most expensive KAYAK. Accommodation: Hostels cost roughly ₹5,000–₹7,000 per night, while hotels range from ₹15,000–₹20,000. Staying in smaller villages like Chandos is cheaper than tourist hubs like Interlaken. Transportation: A 7-day Swiss Travel Pass is highly recommended, as it covers trains, buses, and boats. Food: Restaurants are expensive (approx. ₹1,600 per meal). Saving money is possible by preparing meals and using supermarkets. Activities: Many hikes are free, while specialized mountain excursions (e.g., cable cars to Jungfraujoch) cost ₹5,000–₹8,000 per trip.
